Stop Thinking Like an Employee.
Start Thinking Like a Problem-Solver.

Coach Eshun's 8-step blueprint for rewiring your mindset and building wealth in Africa — starting with what you already have

Coach Eshun was a broke pastor in Oda. No business. No investors. No plan. He rewired his mind through 1,500 books, sold his TV, left his comfort zone, and built a coaching business that generated $10,000 in two weeks. The shift wasn't luck. It was a deliberate sequence of decisions — and this checklist maps every one of them.

The Blueprint — 8 Steps
Phase 1 — Identity
01

Find out what is already inside you

Before capital, before business plans — Coach Eshun says you must answer the question: what specific gift or skill do you have that can solve a problem for someone else? Write it down. That is your starting point. "Your identity is your strength in battle."

02

Invest in your mind before you invest money

Eshun read over 1,500 books to build the capacity to earn. You don't need 1,500 — but you need to start. Pick one business or self-development book this week and read it before making any financial decisions. Knowledge before capital means knowledge protects your capital when things go wrong.

Phase 2 — Environment
03

Audit your social circle — right now

Open your WhatsApp. Look at your most active groups. If the conversations are about sports, gossip, or the past — mute them. Eshun says you cannot build a future with people who are mentally living in 2015. This isn't about being arrogant. It's about protecting your direction.

04

Identify and eliminate your biggest time-waster

Football. TV series. Endless scrolling. Pick the one thing eating the most hours of your week and cut it for 30 days. Eshun stopped watching television entirely. He used that time to read Tony Robbins' 400-page book on a bus to Nigeria while everyone else was watching movies.

Phase 3 — Capital & Action
05

Raise your own startup capital — sell something

Stop waiting for a loan or a "destiny helper." Look at what you own right now that you can convert to startup capital. Eshun sold his television. He's coached people who sold their iPhone 13 Pro Max. An asset sitting in your pocket is capital you haven't deployed yet.

06

Train your eyes to see problems as money

"Money hides in dirty places." Walk around your neighbourhood this week and write down three things that are broken, missing, or frustrating people. Each one is a potential business. Eshun built his entire coaching career from the problem of broke, directionless young Africans — a problem hiding in plain sight.

07

Turn your phone from entertainment to income

The same phone you use to watch "beef" on TikTok can reach thousands of paying customers. This week, create one piece of content on any platform that teaches something you already know. Eshun moved his coaching from radio — which he paid for out of pocket — to social media, and made $10,000 in two weeks.

08

Build a business before you build a house

An uncompleted building generates zero income. A running business generates compounding returns. If you have money earmarked for construction — ask yourself honestly if that capital would grow faster in a business first. Lock up your capital in bricks and you've locked up your future with it.
